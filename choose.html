How to Choose Your Path

Finding the perfect arena for your child's skill level.

Assess Your Child's Starting Point

We break down our curriculum into ranks so students never feel overwhelmed, yet stay constantly challenged. Here is how to pick:

New to Coding?

If your student is in Elementary or early Middle School and has never written a script, they should start as a Squire or Scout. They will learn logic patterns visually before wrestling with typing complex syntax.

Has Basic Experience?

If your child has played with Scratch or built fundamental mods, they are ready for the text-based syntax rules of our Vanguard Track using Python.

High School & AP Prep?

Older or advanced tech students leveling up for college computer science engineering belong in the advanced Paladin or Centurian Tracks studying JavaScript and deep object-oriented C++ software patterns.
